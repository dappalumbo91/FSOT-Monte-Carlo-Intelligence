## 3. Why not “just train an LLM on the archive”?

LLMs excel at language. They do **not** provide:

- a pinned zero-parameter physical law  
- multipath observer collapse as an ontology of thought  
- fail-closed empirical green gates  
- preregistered kill criteria  

This system **uses** language (chew, relay, mind answers) as a surface over a **lawful fluid engine**. Training/adaptation means:

1. Ingest observation (text, API, multipath result)  
2. Map to domain folds  
3. Re-simulate under fixed seeds  
4. Solidify memory when bars clear  
5. Bias future routing / discovery — **not** move \(K\), \(\mathrm{Poof}\), or seeds  

That is the FSOT-derived answer to “how do we learn like an LLM but stay a ToE?”
